About me

4th june 2024

Kia Ora, I'm Shae. Wellington is where I currently reside, but let me tell you about my heart's home: Taranaki. (Hell yeah, Taranaki Hardcore) The Patea River holds a special place in my soul; it runs through my hometown, Stratford. As a kid, my friends and I practically lived in its waters, especially when those hot summers hit. When I was just four, my nan would take my sister and me across Taranaki, showing the very essence of her upbringing, sharing the tales of significance and the deep-rooted history of each spot. Our adventures took us to beaches, rivers, and into the heart of the 'wop wops'(that's what we call the lush countryside, thick with trees and bushes) Those journeys kept me grounded, nurturing my Hauora.

P.S I love you Nana, thank you for making me the man I am today. <3

Let's dive into music, something that's deeply meaningful to me. It's been shaped by the diverse influences of my upbringing and surroundings. From my aunt's R&B vibes to the catchy tunes of Backstreet Boys and NSYNC courtesy of my grandma, music has always been a part of my Life. Those car rides to visit family, with Backstreet Boys blaring and all of us singing along, are memories I hold dear. As I grew, I began to explore my own music taste, discovering artists like Ne-Yo, Akon, TLC, Usher and many more. My primary school days were marked by a little Jonas Brothers obsession, which unexpectedly opened the door to the vibrant world of K-pop in my final year of primary. In 2014, BTS and other groups became a significant part of my playlist.Throughout my teenage years, my music palette expanded to a variety of genres: K-pop, R&B, K-R&B pop, rap, Latin beats, J-pop, rock, and reggae. Nowadays, my go-to genre is R&B, with a little bit of K-pop and an big mix of other genres. SZA, Jungkook and Khamari currently top my list of favorites.But let's change gears. If you've read this far, I'd love to hear your song recommendations. I'm always up for discovering new music!
I also influenced my blog around spotify, to show the love of listening to music.
