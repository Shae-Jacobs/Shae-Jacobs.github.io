Identity and Values

17th june 2024

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

An ethical decision I faced involved making the decision to emotionally distance myself from one of my aunts. Her actions in a this situation conflicted with my moral values and caused me pain, especially since we had been close during my upbringing. After carefully considering my values, I made the difficult choice to reduce contact with her. It was a painful decision because it meant severing an important relationship for my personal growth. Reflecting on this decision, I believe it was better for my well-being. Even if she apologizes and changes her behavior in the future, rebuilding that trust and forgiveness will take time.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Being raised by my Nan has shaped my values and identity a lot. As a Jehovah's Witness, she instilled in me the importance of never judging others, practicing forgiveness, and treating everyone equally through our regular church attendance. Although I no longer attend church, those years engraved important values. My relationship with my mother has been complex to say the least. Initially unable to care for me adequately, she later re-entered my life. Despite past hardships and the challenging environment she put me through, this period taught me resilience and set standards for myself. Learning about her struggles with addiction gave me empathy, encouraging me to prioritize understanding over personal grievances. Empathy and understanding are values I hold close to my heart. Not having a father figure was challenging, leaving me to question my identity and cultural roots. The absence of paternal guidance forced me to navigate self-discovery independently, which was to say the least... hard. Growing up alongside my older sister, who always looked out for me whether at school or in public, was truly endearing. She consistently aimed for academic excellence and held a high social status, which sometimes made me feel inadequate and envious. However, as I matured, I began to want to achieve more and expand my own goals. My sister's influence taught me the importance of cherishing family bonds and maintaining determination towards my goals. I am grateful to my loving Nan and Older sister for providing stability and guidance during this important time in my life.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

My strengths lie in my easy going nature; while I may come across as shy, I easily warm up to people as I get to understand and know them better. Finding a frequency within myself that resonates with them, this makes for effective collaborations in pair and group projects. Another strength of mine is my empathetic nature; I am always mindfull of others feelings and circumstances, which enhances my ability to support my peers. Additionally, my strong determination drives me to constantly learn and grow, making me understand more in my studies.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

One limitation I face is perfectionism. I have a strong desire for things to meet my standards, which can sometimes lead to me burning out on specific aspects of tasks. This can result in stress or procrastination as I work for perfection. This could impact my learning journey by causing me being burnout or falling behind my peers.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

In a previous job, I often found myself working alongside new team members due to turnover. There was some resistance as I took on the role of leadership and worked to foster teamwork among us. Often, I was younger than them, which made it challenging to assert myself. I approached the situation with patience, shouldering most of the workload until they became more proficient. I focused on finding common ground and engaging them in comfortable conversations while we worked together. Reflecting on this experience, I realize I should have been more assertive in my communication. I believe that asserting myself better could have sped up their improvement and enhanced our teamwork sooner.
